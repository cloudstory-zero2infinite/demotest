## 1 PURPOSE

The purpose of this Information Security Policy is to establish rules for protecting the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of the organization’s information assets. This policy provides the highest level of authority in guiding the establishment, implementation, maintenance, and continuous improvement of the Information Security Management System (ISMS). <test>

## 2 SCOPE

This policy applies to all members of the organization as defined in the scope of the ISMS.

## 3 TERMS AND DEFINITIONS

* **Confidentiality:** The property that information is not made available or disclosed to unauthorized individuals, entities, or processes.
* **Information Security:** The preservation of confidentiality, integrity, and availability of information.
* **Integrity:** The property of accuracy and completeness.
* **Availability:** The property of being accessible and usable upon demand by an authorized entity.
* **ISMS:** Information security management system.
* **CISO:** Chief Information Security Officer.

## 6 POLICY

### 6.1 Commitment

Our organization is committed to maintaining the highest standards of information security by complying with all applicable legal, regulatory, and contractual requirements related to the protection of information assets. We pledge to continuously monitor and review our security practices to ensure they meet or exceed the requirements set forth by relevant standards and regulations. This commitment extends to all employees, partners, and stakeholders, who are expected to adhere to our information security policies and procedures, ensuring the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of our information assets are always protected.

In support of these commitments, our organization will:

* **Establish and Maintain a Comprehensive Information Security Policy:** We will develop, implement, and continually update a set of policies that aligns with industry standards, regulatory requirements, and the organization’s strategic objectives.
* **Define and Communicate Security Objectives:** Measurable security objectives will be set to support our strategic goals, with clear communication to all relevant stakeholders to ensure alignment and understanding.
* **Allocate Necessary Resources:** We will provide the financial, technological, and human resources required to effectively implement and sustain our Information Security Management System (ISMS).
* **Enhance Employee Awareness and Training:** All employees will receive ongoing support and training to understand their information security responsibilities and to contribute to a secure working environment.
* **Foster a Culture of Security:** A proactive security culture will be promoted throughout the organization, encouraging all individuals to prioritize and actively participate in the protection of information assets.
* **Commit to Continuous Improvement:** We will regularly review and refine our information security policies, procedures, and controls to ensure their effectiveness and relevance, staying responsive to emerging threats and changes in the regulatory landscape.
* **Implement Robust Risk Management:** A comprehensive risk management framework will be employed to identify, assess, and mitigate information security risks. Appropriate controls will be applied based on regular risk assessments.
* **Ensure Effective Communication:** Open and effective communication channels will be maintained to keep all stakeholders informed about our information security policies, procedures, and any updates or changes.
* **Support Information Security Management Roles:** We will empower relevant management roles by providing the necessary authority, resources, and guidance to ensure they can effectively fulfil their responsibilities in maintaining the security of our information assets.

### 6.2 Information security domains & objectives

Our organization is committed to maintaining a robust ISMS that supports business objectives through the following ISMS domains and their associated security objectives [security objectives are defined in the document `IT-ISMS-POL-010_ISMS InfoSec Objectives_Simplify3x_V1.0.docx`].

**1) Threat and Vulnerability Defense Management**
Establish and maintain integrated processes to identify, assess, and mitigate vulnerabilities, malware risks, and emerging threat actors by leveraging threat intelligence, continuous monitoring, and timely remediation actions to protect organizational information assets and systems.

**2) Asset Inventory and Configuration Management**
Establish and maintain a comprehensive asset inventory and configuration management capability to ensure accurate identification, ownership, secure configuration, and effective lifecycle management of all information assets within the ISMS scope.

**3) Information Classification and Labelling**
Establish and maintain an information classification and labelling framework in accordance with ISO/IEC 27001 principles to ensure information is identified, classified, and protected based on its sensitivity, criticality, and business value, and handled in line with the process defined in process document - `IT-ISMS-PRO-002_ISMS Information Classification and Labelling_Simplify3x-V1.0`.

**4) Information Transfer Security**
Establish and maintain information transfer rules, procedures, and agreements to ensure secure handling and protection of information across all transfer facilities within the organization and between the organization and external parties.

**5) Access Control and Identity Management**
Establish and maintain access control and identity management practices to ensure that access to information and systems is authorized, appropriate, and aligned with defined roles and business requirements.

**6) Software Development and Application Security**
Establish and maintain secure software development and application security practices by embedding security requirements across the SDLC, applying secure-by-design and secure system architecture and engineering principles, and enforcing secure coding and security testing activities. Ensure appropriate segregation of development, testing, and production environments to reduce risk and prevent unauthorized changes.

**7) Incident Management and Business Continuity Resilience**
Establish and maintain incident management and business continuity practices to ensure timely identification, response, recovery, and continuity of critical operations during disruptive events. Ensure redundancy and resilience of information processing facilities to support availability and minimize the impact of security incidents or operational disruptions.

**8) Independent Review, Compliance, and SOP Governance**
Establish and maintain independent review mechanisms to periodically assess information security practices, regulatory compliance, and adherence to defined policies and standard operating procedures. Ensure identified gaps are documented, reported, and remediated to support continual improvement of the ISMS.

**9) Network Security and Cryptography**
Establish and maintain controls to ensure networks, network devices, and network services are securely configured, managed, and monitored to protect information across systems and applications. Implement network segregation and web filtering mechanisms to reduce unauthorized access and exposure to malicious content. Define and enforce the effective use of cryptography, including secure cryptographic key management, to protect information at rest and in transit.

### 6.3 Communication

The information security policy shall be communicated to all persons within the scope of the ISMS by emails, Security awareness videos from Top management, Inclusion in Mandatory training and assessment, Posters/flyers On premises, ISMS steering committee meetings etc.

This policy may be shared and communicated with external interested parties.

## 7 COMPLIANCE

### 7.1 Measurement

The organization will ensure compliance with this policy through various methods, including management reviews, internal and external audits, established KPI and metrics defined in `IT-ISMS-POL-010_ISMS InfoSec Objectives_Simplify3x_V1.0.docx` and feedback from employees and stakeholders.

### 7.2 Exceptions

Any exception to this policy must be reviewed by CISO and approved by CTO in advance. The process for requesting an exception involves submitting a detailed justification, including the reasons for the exception, the potential risks, and the proposed mitigation measures. The ISO Office will thoroughly evaluate the request to ensure that it does not compromise the overall security posture of the organization. Only upon receiving explicit approval from CTO will the exception be considered valid and enforceable.

### 7.3 Violations

Members of the organization found to have violated this policy may be subject to disciplinary action. Depending on the severity of the violation, consequences may include mandatory retraining on information security policies, formal reprimands, or suspension. The organization will thoroughly investigate any suspected policy violations to ensure fairness and accuracy in the disciplinary process.
